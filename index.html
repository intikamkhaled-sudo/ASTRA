SPACE SUSTAINABILITY

Built for a Sustainable Future in Orbit

Our project is designed to support space sustainability throughout the entire satellite life cycle — from design and operation to safe end-of-life disposal. The CubeSat uses advanced technologies such as Artificial Intelligence, robotic systems, magnetic handling mechanisms, smart nets, and an Electrodynamic Tether to monitor, detect, and reduce space debris while improving mission efficiency and orbital safety.

⚡

Smart Energy Management

The system minimizes energy consumption through smart resource management and adaptive tether control — the tether extends only when required to reduce risks and improve efficiency.

◉

AI-Driven Autonomy

The AI system analyzes debris danger levels, selects suitable handling methods, and autonomously controls mission operations to reduce unnecessary resource usage.

⬡

Long-Term Orbital Safety

The mission helps reduce debris accumulation, minimize collision risks, and prevent the generation of new debris — supporting long-term orbital sustainability.

⟳

Zero Debris End-of-Life

At mission end, the satellite performs a controlled deorbit maneuver and safely burns during atmospheric reentry — ensuring zero additional debris remains in orbit.

The project follows international space sustainability and orbital debris mitigation principles, supporting responsible space operations and contributing to a safer and more sustainable future for space exploration.

HOW IT WORKS

Mission Flow

1

Detection

AI camera onboard the satellite identifies debris size, trajectory, and velocity in real time.

2

Classification

AI classifies the debris — small targets activate the net system, large targets trigger the robotic arm.

3

Capture

The appropriate system deploys and secures the debris using either net wrapping or electromagnetic grip.

4

Deorbit & Burn

Captured debris is redirected toward Earth's atmosphere where it burns up harmlessly upon reentry.
